This is one of my all time favorite Batman graphic novels. It successfully tells a story of a young struggling Batman and his journey to fight crime while at the same time being totally new to everything. Batman doesn't have any special gadgets or cool tricks in here. He struggles staying afloat but his heart is in a good place. We see Batman at his purest human form as he is deliberately putting himself at risk for random citizens in Gotham. It also explores the character of Commisioner Gordon, as in this story he is completely new to Gotham City and he isn't commisioner yet. This story does a well job of exploring every character and their own humanity.

























































This is another really good Batman story that takes place throughout an entire year. In this story, Batman is more experienced, he has multiple enemies as well as allies and he knows how to use every single character to his advantange. The Long Halloween explores a new enemy that Batman is facing which happens to be a serial killer that is after members of the mob. We also get to see some of Batman's most iconic enemies such The Joker, Catwoman, Scarecrow, The Riddler and more. This graphic novel does a good job of taking its pace and has multiple plot twists. The reader is given a chance to go through this case step by step with everyone else in the story and it fully brings you in the role of a detective.

























































A totally modern and new take on Batman and a really good introduction to DC's New 52. This is the first issue of a new collection of Batman stories. In here Batman is fully experienced and has the support of his Bat-Family. Batman is thrown into a complex conspiracy theory surrounding Gotham and its most prominent historical figures. Batman faces an organization that makes him struggle with his own identity and connection with his city. Batman being in the game for some time now has really good skills along with amazing technological gadgets.
